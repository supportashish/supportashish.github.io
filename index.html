About Ashish

Ashish Pokharel is a beloved son, fiancé, and friend who has touched the lives of everyone around him with his warmth, humor, and generous spirit.

On Monday, March 16, Ashish suffered a stroke and has been in the ICU at Hudson Regional Hospital since. The cause is still unknown — he has been and will continue to undergo tests to determine a diagnosis. He will be in inpatient care for approximately two weeks and will need ongoing speech and physical therapy over the next few months.

While the road ahead is long, Ashish is a fighter — and with the support of his mom and his fiancée Van by his side, he's already making progress every day.

However, the recovery process has created significant challenges for the family. Between hospital stays, medical expenses, and daily needs, they need our help. Whether it's a donation, a meal, a ride, or simply visiting and lifting his spirits — every act of kindness makes a difference.

This page is here to coordinate how we, as a community, can rally around Ashish and his family during this time.

📍 Ashish lives in Jersey City, NJ and is currently at Hudson Regional Hospital in Secaucus, NJ.

ICU Visitation Policy

  • Two (2) visitors are permitted at a time unless approved by the patient's care team.
  • Children under 18 are not permitted to visit without permission from the patient's care team.
  • All ICU visitors are required to stop at the security desk in the main lobby and obtain a visitor badge.
  • All visitors shall remain in the 3rd floor waiting room area when not permitted in the ICU.
  • Upon arrival to the ICU, visitors will be directed to the 3rd floor waiting room area if it is an inconvenient time to see the patient. Visitors will be informed by the patient's care team when visiting will be permitted.
  • If visiting is restricted, the patient's care team will update the family and/or significant other of the patient's condition at least every hour.

Visitors may not be permitted or may be asked to leave:

  • When deemed in the best interest of the patient
  • Upon request by the patient
  • In emergency situations
